Usability

Usability is a set of rules to measure the quality of a user experience of your website with focus on Effectiveness, Efficiency and Satisfaction. Among the benefit of usability, we can find: web services and websites more effective, increased customer satisfaction, decreased post-implementation changes and is not too hard, or expensive, or onerous.

10 Usabilit Heuristics, by Jakob Nielsen

1. Visibility of system status

Systems may keeps users informed about what is going on with feedbacks.

2. Match between system and real world

The system shold use the user's language insted system-oriented terms.

3. User control and freedom

Users needs to have a kind of "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state by mistake.

4. Consistency and standards

Users doesn't wonder if different words, situations or otheres, means the same thing.

5. Error prevention

A careful design which prevents a problem before occurred.

6. Recognition rather than recall

Instructions for use of system should be visible or easily retrievable whenever appropriate.

7. Flexibility and efficiency of use

Allow users to tailor frequent actions.

8. Aesthetic and minimalist design

Only importantes and direct information.

9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors

Error messages should indicate the problem and suggest a solution.

10. Help and documentation

It may be necessary to provide help and documentation, not too large and easy to search for information.
